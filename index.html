Making Jewelry!

Description

Making Jewelry is so fun!

It's a hobby that you can do anywhere, almost any time. Pretty accessible too: you only need some string to get started!

I make jewelry out of three main mediums: beads, string, and elastic bands. I've been making friendship bracelets out of string for a long time, and started when I was 8 years old at summer camp. For elastics, I use a rainbow loom. I also used that when I was younger, but I recently started back up again. Lastly, I use beads! This is the most versatile one in my opinion. You can make earrings, necklaces, bracelets, anklets, rings, whatever you can think of! My favorite thing to do with them is to look up a bead animal pattern on pinterest and then recreate it with wire and seed beads so I can hang them on earrings.

To the top right, there are some basic examples. I will go into more detail in the sections below!

String - Bracelets/Anklets

Making bracelets and anklets out of string has long been a part of my artistic expression. When I was at camp we would make them for each other, attatching them to our water bottles. That is essentially how I still make them today!

Now, however, I can make more complicated patterns than I used to. My current favorites to make are the chevron, diamond, and vertical wave patterns. If I want to relax and zone out while making a bracelet, I'll typically do the simpler candy stripe.

Here is a linked video tutorial for a candy stripe bracelet!

Beads

Beads are currently my favorite medium to use! They are also the most versatile medium to use (in my opinion). The very first thing I learned to make out of beads was a flower patterned necklace, and it is still one of my favorite things to make. It is, however, a lot more time consuming than making a bracelet.

Something that ISN'T as time consuming - but just as fun - is using tiny glass seed beads to make bead animals! If you follow a pattern on pinterest, they're the PERFECT size to attach to earrings or a keychain.
